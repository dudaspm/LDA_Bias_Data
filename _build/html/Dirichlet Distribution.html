When All Possibility Are Equally Likely

In situations where are all possibilities are equally likely (equally likely to sit at a table with someone else (âšª&ðŸ˜ƒ) or sit at a new table (âšª)), we can abbreviate this to a simple probablity:

\(\frac{ðŸ˜ƒ}{ðŸ˜ƒðŸ˜ƒðŸ˜ƒðŸ˜ƒðŸ¤”}\) \( = \) \(\frac{\text{Number of people sitting at table (âšª&ðŸ˜ƒ)}}{\text{All people (ðŸ˜ƒðŸ˜ƒðŸ˜ƒðŸ˜ƒðŸ¤”)}}\) \( = \) \(\frac{N_j}{N}\)

AND

\(\frac{ðŸ¤”}{ðŸ˜ƒðŸ˜ƒðŸ˜ƒðŸ˜ƒðŸ¤”}\) \( = \) \(\frac{\text{Number of people who can sit at a new table (âšª)}}{\text{All people (ðŸ˜ƒðŸ˜ƒðŸ˜ƒðŸ˜ƒðŸ¤”)}}\) \( = \) \(\frac{1}{N}\)

To Join Or Not To Join

As shown in the animation, there are two conditions: to join or not to join. Both of these take advantage of the \(\frac{N_j}{N}\) relationship, but it can be seen that as tables are filled, the more likely this could occur. Meaningâ€¦

To join

\(\frac{ðŸ˜ƒ}{ðŸ˜ƒðŸ˜ƒðŸ˜ƒðŸ˜ƒðŸ¤”}\) \( + \) \(\frac{ðŸ˜ƒ}{ðŸ˜ƒðŸ˜ƒðŸ˜ƒðŸ˜ƒðŸ¤”}\) \( + \) \(\frac{ðŸ˜ƒ}{ðŸ˜ƒðŸ˜ƒðŸ˜ƒðŸ˜ƒðŸ¤”}\) \( + \) \(\frac{ðŸ˜ƒ}{ðŸ˜ƒðŸ˜ƒðŸ˜ƒðŸ˜ƒðŸ¤”}\) \( + \) \( = \) \(\frac{N-1}{N}\)

Or Not To Join

\(\frac{ðŸ¤”}{ðŸ˜ƒðŸ˜ƒðŸ˜ƒðŸ˜ƒðŸ¤”}\) \( = \) \(\frac{1}{N}\)

or, even better

The Predictive Probability

The Dirichlet has the predicitve probability of

\(\frac{N_j}{N+p} + \frac{p}{N+p} = 1\)

Where, traditionally, you will see p written as alpha (\(\alpha\)).

Soâ€¦ \(\frac{N_j}{N+\alpha} + \frac{\alpha}{N+\alpha} = 1\)

When dealing with the Dirichlet process, we are dealing with probabilities. As before, p or \(\alpha\), represents a probability. In this case, the person entering will more than likely want to sit at a table and, more specifically, the table with the most people. This will rarely be as extreme as â€œThe Social Butterfly,â€ but instead be represented by the \(\frac{N_j}{N+\alpha} + \frac{\alpha}{N+\alpha} = 1\) for each table.
